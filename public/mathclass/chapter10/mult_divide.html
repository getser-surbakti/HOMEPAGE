2. There are 10 packets of nuts in a box. The mass of each packet is 150 g.

a. A label on the box states that the mass of the 10 packets of nuts is 1 kg. Do you think the label is correct? Why do you say so?

No, the label is not correct. The total mass of the 10 packets of nuts is 10 × 150 g = 1500 g, which is 1.5 kg.

b. There are 100 boxes of nuts in a store. Describe how you would find the total mass of the nuts in the store. Convince your partner.

To find the total mass of the nuts in the store, multiply the mass of one box by the number of boxes. Since one box has a mass of 1.5 kg, the total mass is 100 × 1.5 kg = 150 kg.

3. A dairy farm sold 55,500 ml of milk in 1 week.

If the milk was sold in 1-litre and 500-ml milk cartons, what is the greatest possible number of 1-litre milk cartons sold?

To maximize the number of 1-litre milk cartons, use as many 1-litre cartons as possible. Since 1 litre = 1000 ml, the greatest number of 1-litre cartons is 55,500 ml ÷ 1000 ml = 55 cartons. The remaining 500 ml can be sold in one 500-ml carton.
