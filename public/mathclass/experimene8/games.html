Game 1: Probability Dice Roll

Objective: Teams compete to predict the outcome of dice rolls.

How to Play:

  • You will have 2 minutes to decide
  • Each team chooses a card (Less than 6/Greater than 6) they think will appear on a dice roll.
  • The dice will be rolled 10 times
  • You will have a chance to switch after 5 rolls/li>

Game 2: Coin Flip Prediction Challenge

Objective: Teams predict the outcome of multiple coin flips.

How to Play:

  • Each team chooses either "heads" or "tails" before each flip.
  • A coin is flipped 10 times, and the team that correctly predicts the most flips wins.
  • You will need to choose more tails/more heads
  • You have 2 minutes to decide
  • Good Luck

Game 3: Lucky Draw Challenge

Objective: Teams compete to draw certain colored balls from a bag.

How to Play:

  • Fill a bag with colored balls (e.g., red, blue, yellow, green), with a different number of balls for each color.
  • Teams take turns drawing a ball from the bag, with the goal of drawing a certain color.
  • After a series of draws, the team that draws the most of their chosen color wins.
  • This game can help teach concepts of probability and chance.

Game 4: Estimation Station

Objective: Teams estimate quantities and compare their predictions with actual results.

How to Play:

  • Prepare several containers with a known number of small objects (e.g., beans, marbles, or buttons).
  • Teams take turns estimating how many objects are in each container.
  • After all estimates are made, the actual number is revealed, and the team with the closest guess wins.
  • This helps students practice making informed estimates based on probability.
